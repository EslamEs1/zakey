Commercial

Every Detail Sells

Ambience that elevates the product. Automation that protects the margin.

The Zakey Smart Retail solution turns a boutique, showroom, or flagship store into a curated sensory environment — automated spotlight scenes that highlight key merchandise, climate precision that keeps browsers comfortable without staff intervention, and energy monitoring that turns operational savings into competitive advantage.

The challenge

Why retail spaces need smarter control

  1. Lighting that doesn't match the moment

    A boutique runs a single lighting scene all day — too bright during the intimate afternoon lull, too dim under the evening footfall peak. Premium merchandise deserves presentation lighting that adapts to time of day, occupancy level, and promotional priority. Static lighting leaves product value on the floor.

  2. Energy costs that compound invisibly

    Display lighting, HVAC, and security systems run at full capacity from open to close regardless of footfall. In quiet hours, this represents pure waste — but without monitoring and automation, there is no visibility and no action. Retail energy bills balloon without a single dramatic event to prompt review.

  3. Open and close routines that consume staff time

    The morning scene — unlocking, powering up displays, setting the HVAC, adjusting lighting zones — takes ten to fifteen minutes of focused staff time every day. The after-hours lockdown adds another ten. Across a year, that is days of avoidable operational cost for a process that should be one tap.

  4. After-hours security that requires manual checks

    Door, window, and motion sensors from separate security systems generate alerts to separate apps, require separate monitoring subscriptions, and often create so many notifications that genuine events are buried in noise. A unified system that integrates security into the same interface as lighting and climate changes the reliability of the response.

The Zakey answer

One tap opens the store. One tap locks it.

Zakey unifies lighting, climate, security, and energy monitoring under one system for the retail environment. The Open-Doors Scene activates at the configured store opening time — or with a tap from the manager's phone while still in the car: track lights step up to display intensity, HVAC reaches target temperature, display screens come live, and the Smart Door Lock disengages. No walk-through routine, no checklist, no waiting for systems to warm up.

Throughout the day, footfall sensors adjust HVAC load and secondary lighting zones in response to occupancy — full intensity during peak hours, a quiet 35% energy-saving mode during the slow afternoon. After closing, the After-Hours Lockdown scene secures the perimeter, steps lighting down to security level, and begins energy conservation automatically. Any motion inside after hours triggers an immediate app alert, not a separate alarm system notification.

What's inside

Six systems, one premium space

  • Lighting Control

    The Lighting Controller manages ceiling track spots, display shelf LEDs, window accent strips, and general ambient zones independently. Each zone is dimmable and colour-temperature adjustable. Promotional spotlights can be queued to a higher intensity for featured merchandise — then restored automatically when the promotion ends. All scenes persist across power cycles without reprogramming.

  • Climate Control

    The Climate Hub maintains a consistent, comfortable store temperature that keeps browsers lingering without the excessive cooling that signals an empty shop. Occupancy sensors feed real-time footfall data to the HVAC logic: high footfall means more cooling capacity, slow hours mean a reduced setpoint that saves energy without sacrificing comfort for the few customers present. Air quality monitoring flags CO₂ build-up during busy periods.

  • Security

    Smart Door Lock, door and window sensors, and indoor cameras integrate into the same Zakey App interface as lighting and climate. After-hours motion triggers a layered alert: immediate app notification, then a time-lapse camera capture. During trading hours, the Smart Door Lock logs every entry and exit — useful for compliance, insurance, and staff management without requiring a separate access control system.

  • Sensors

    Presence and footfall sensors at the entrance and across the floor plan provide real-time occupancy data that drives lighting and HVAC decisions. Door and window contact sensors form the perimeter detection layer. Environment sensors monitor temperature, humidity, and air quality continuously — ensuring that storage areas and display cases remain within specification for sensitive merchandise.

  • Energy Monitoring

    The Energy Meter tracks consumption by circuit — lighting, HVAC, displays, and general equipment — giving the manager a clear breakdown of where the power bill actually comes from. Energy-saving mode reduces lighting to 30% and HVAC to a maintenance setpoint during documented slow periods. Monthly reports show the impact of automation on operating costs — a number worth showing to ownership or franchisors.

  • App & Central Control

    The Zakey App gives the store manager full remote control — activate Open-Doors or After-Hours Lockdown from anywhere, check live energy usage, review security event logs, and adjust lighting scenes without being on-site. The Aura Panel wall controller in the staff area provides instant one-touch scene access for floor staff without requiring them to open an app in front of customers.

Live scenarios

Scenes that run the store

Open-Doors Mode

At 9:55 AM — five minutes before opening — the manager taps Open-Doors from the Zakey App. Track spots step up to 85% at 3000 K warmth, display shelf LEDs activate, window accent strips come on at 60%, HVAC reaches target temperature, and the Smart Door Lock disengages for the trading day. The store is ready before the first customer arrives, without a single manual step on the floor.

Slow-Hour Energy-Saver

Between 1:00 PM and 3:30 PM, footfall sensors register low occupancy. The Zakey system automatically transitions to Slow-Hour mode: display shelf LEDs step to 55%, secondary ambient zones dim to 40%, and HVAC reduces capacity by 20% while maintaining a comfortable temperature for the customers present. Staff don't notice, customers don't notice — but energy consumption drops by roughly 28% for those two and a half hours.

After-Hours Lockdown

The last staff member taps After-Hours from the Aura Panel near the exit. The Smart Door Lock engages, all display lighting steps to 5% security level, HVAC drops to an energy-conservation setpoint, and the security monitoring profile activates on every door, window, and interior motion sensor. Any triggered sensor sends an immediate app notification with the sensor location — no separate alarm subscription, no call centre relay, direct to the manager's phone.

Why it matters

Built around the bottom line and the experience

  • Premium Ambience, Always On

    Automated lighting scenes ensure the store looks its best at opening, throughout trading, and during promotional moments — without relying on staff to remember the setup each morning.

  • Measurable Energy Savings

    Occupancy-driven lighting and HVAC automation reduces energy consumption by 20–35% during slow trading periods — reported automatically in the Zakey App with zero manual data collection.

  • Unified Security

    Door sensors, motion detection, camera alerts, and lock access logging in one interface eliminates separate security subscriptions and the missed-alert risk of fragmented systems.

  • Remote Management

    The store manager opens, adjusts, and locks the store remotely from any location. A multi-location operator can manage every site from one Zakey App account without site visits.

  • Premium Customer Experience

    Consistent temperature, flattering lighting, and a calm, well-maintained environment keep browsers comfortable and in-store longer — directly correlated with higher conversion in premium retail.

  • Operational Time Recovery

    Open and close routines that took fifteen minutes of staff time each direction become single-tap scenes. Over a year, this recovers meaningful hours that return to customer service or sales work.
