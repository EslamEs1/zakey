Lifestyle

The Arena Belongs to You

One scene trigger transforms your gaming room into a world of its own.

The Zakey Gaming Room brings RGB ambient control, climate precision, and scenario automation into a single interface — no app-hopping, no interruptions, just pure immersion from the moment you sit down. Cinema Mode, Tournament Mode, Late-Night Mode — all activated by a single tap or a single word.

The challenge

Why gaming rooms deserve smarter control

  1. Scene transitions that shatter immersion

    Switching from a competitive shooter to a movie requires adjusting RGB strips, reconfiguring the thermostat, and dimming ceiling lights — manually, in three different apps. Every transition pulls you out of the experience before it even begins.

  2. GPU heat goes unchecked

    High-end GPUs and dense equipment generate significant heat during extended sessions. Without climate automation, the room warms steadily until performance degrades — or you pause the game to lower the thermostat yourself.

  3. Lighting and audio that can't sync to the moment

    Static lighting presets and ambient strips with no scene memory mean every session starts with manual tweaking — or you leave everything at a generic default that serves no mode particularly well.

  4. Fragmented control across too many apps

    RGB controllers, smart bulbs, thermostats, and entertainment devices each live in separate ecosystems. Gaming sessions shouldn't begin with a setup routine across four different apps before the first match loads.

  5. Late-night ambience that wakes the household

    Reactive RGB lighting and bright overhead strips look great during peak hours but bleed light under doors and into hallways. A thoughtful late-night mode that dims and quiets automatically shouldn't require a manual ritual at midnight.

The Zakey answer

One remote, one scene, total immersion

Zakey connects the Ambient Strip Controller, Climate Hub, Sense Engine sensors, and Aura Panel into one cohesive scene system. Choose a mode from the Scene Remote on your desk — or say it aloud — and every component shifts in unison: bias lighting dims behind the display, RGB strips shift colour temperature, the Climate Hub adjusts to your preferred session setpoint, and presence sensors keep everything alive for exactly as long as you're in the chair.

There are no mode conflicts. Cinema Mode knows you're watching and hands audio presence to the room. Tournament Mode knows you're competing and locks the room into focus conditions. Late-Night Mode reads the hour and your occupancy to give you full immersion without disturbing anyone else. All of it learned once, automated forever.

What's inside

Five systems, one immersive room

  • Ambient Lighting

    The Ambient Strip Controller governs bias lighting behind your display, ceiling wash LED, and floor accent strips — all scene-aware, dimmable to 1%, and shift-ready in under 400 ms. No colour temperature ever repeats across modes.

  • Climate Control

    The Climate Hub monitors room temperature in real time and adjusts AC setpoint automatically as GPU and CPU load climbs. Tournament Mode targets 19 °C for cognitive clarity; Cinema Mode steps up 2 °C for comfort during passive viewing.

  • Sensors

    Presence sensors know when you leave the chair and begin an idle countdown — auto-shutting lighting and HVAC after ten minutes. Temperature and air quality sensors feed the Climate Hub continuously, ensuring comfort stays on target throughout long sessions.

  • App & Voice

    The Zakey App and full Google Assistant and Amazon Alexa support let you trigger any scene hands-free mid-match. "Activate Tournament Mode" or a single tap — both work. The app also exposes fine-grained colour and temperature controls for custom sessions.

  • Central Control

    The Scene Remote or Aura Panel on the desk gives you one-tap access to every preset — no keyboard shortcuts, no task switching, no looking away from the screen. Three physical buttons map to Cinema, Tournament, and Late-Night modes out of the box.

Live scenarios

Every mode, engineered for the moment

Cinema Mode

Bias strips shift to deep red at 12% brightness. Overhead dims to 4%. Climate Hub sets the room to a relaxed 22 °C. Presence sensor holds the scene for the duration — and restores gaming defaults the moment the movie ends and motion resumes at the desk.

Tournament Mode

Strips shift to a cold white-blue at 40% — bright enough for camera visibility, cool enough to avoid eye fatigue over a four-hour session. Climate drops to 19 °C. The Scene Remote soft-locks to prevent accidental mode switches mid-match. Focus, uninterrupted.

Late-Night Mode

All RGB dims to 8% warm amber at 2700 K. Overhead off. Climate steps up to a comfortable 23 °C. The presence sensor holds everything alive while you're in the chair — and fully powers down within five minutes of you leaving, silently and automatically.
