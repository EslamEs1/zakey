Lifestyle

Live Fully, Age Gracefully

Safety and independence, woven gently into everyday life.

The Zakey Elderly Care solution wraps a home in quiet intelligence — motion and presence sensors detect unusual patterns, smart lighting guides safe night movement, and a one-touch scene button brings the whole house into a comfortable state instantly. For residents, it means living confidently. For families, it means sleeping soundly.

The challenge

Why elderly residents need a smarter home

  1. Falls happen in the dark

    A large share of fall incidents occur at night, on the way to the bathroom. Conventional lighting means fumbling for wall switches in the dark, or leaving lights on all night to feel safe — neither is a real solution. Gentle, motion-triggered path lighting changes this fundamentally.

  2. Isolation amplifies risk

    When a resident lives alone or is alone for parts of the day, a fall or medical event can go undetected for hours. Without passive monitoring, families have no way of knowing whether a morning routine is progressing normally until they call — and sometimes that is too late.

  3. Complex controls create barriers

    Thermostats, lighting panels, curtain remotes, and TV controllers each have their own interface, their own logic, their own failures. For a resident with limited mobility or reduced dexterity, managing multiple devices is not just inconvenient — it is exhausting and often abandoned.

  4. Front door security is difficult to manage

    Navigating to the front door to admit a carer, family member, or delivery — especially across a large home — can be a significant physical challenge. And in an emergency, a conventional lock becomes an obstacle for first responders.

  5. Families carry constant background worry

    Without visibility into a loved one's daily patterns, families experience persistent, low-level anxiety — not during crises, but every quiet Tuesday when no one has heard anything by noon. Real peace of mind requires passive signals, not daily check-in calls.

The Zakey answer

Quiet protection, gentle control

Zakey builds a passive safety layer around the resident's daily routine. Motion sensors track presence and movement patterns across rooms — not intrusively, but reliably enough to detect an absence that breaks the normal pattern and alert the family app immediately. Smart locks grant remote access to carers and family with a single tap, and the Scene Button on a side table collapses the entire home into a comfortable, pre-set state without the resident needing to navigate any app or panel.

Lighting works silently in the background: soft night-path strips activate below knee height when motion is detected after midnight, guiding safely without flooding the room. The Climate Hub maintains a steady, comfortable temperature automatically — within a medically appropriate range — removing the need to adjust thermostats. Every device in the ecosystem speaks to every other through an encrypted local mesh, so the system continues operating even without internet.

What's inside

Six quiet systems, one safe home

  • Motion & Presence Sensors

    Passive infrared and presence sensors installed at bedroom door, bathroom entry, and living room detect daily patterns without cameras. If the morning routine stalls — no motion by a configurable hour — the family app receives a quiet alert. Sensors also trigger night-path lighting automatically as the resident moves through the home.

  • Smart Security

    The Smart Door Lock replaces the conventional deadbolt with remote access for family and carers — no keys to lose, no door to rush to. In a medical emergency, the Zakey App grants first responders immediate access via a time-limited code. Door and window sensors complete the perimeter, with quiet alerts rather than alarming sirens.

  • Lighting Control

    Warm, gentle lighting scenes replace harsh overhead glare. Night-path strips along skirting boards and bathroom thresholds activate on presence and deactivate quietly after the resident returns to bed. Morning light brightens gradually rather than snapping on. Every scene is designed around physiological comfort, not technical convenience.

  • App & Voice

    The Zakey App gives family members real-time visibility into the home's status — not surveillance, but reassurance through pattern. The resident gets Google Assistant and Amazon Alexa voice control for hands-free command of lights, locks, and climate. No complex interfaces, no small text, no menu navigation required from the resident's side.

  • Climate Control

    The Climate Hub maintains a steady indoor temperature within a configurable comfort band — warm enough for circulation, never uncomfortably hot. Temperature and air quality sensors detect CO₂ build-up and trigger fresh-air reminders. The resident never needs to adjust a thermostat; the system keeps the home within their preferred range automatically throughout the day.

  • Curtain Automation

    Motorised curtains open gently each morning as part of the Wake-Up scene, admitting natural light that supports healthy circadian rhythm. They close at dusk automatically, removing the need to navigate across the room for daily comfort. The silent track motor operates without any sound that could startle a light sleeper.

Live scenarios

The moments that matter most

Morning Wellness Check

At 7:30 AM, the Wake-Up scene begins: bedroom curtains part slowly, lighting warms from 10% to 60% over fifteen minutes, and the Climate Hub steps up to a comfortable 22 °C. The motion sensor logs the resident's movement to the kitchen — and if that movement hasn't registered by 9:00 AM, the family app receives a gentle wellness prompt rather than an alarm. A quiet signal that the morning is on track.

Fall Detection Response

Motion sensors detect an unusual stationary event — no movement from the bathroom presence sensor for seven minutes at a time the resident is normally active. The Zakey App sends a priority alert to two family contacts immediately. One contact remotely unlocks the front door for a neighbour or carer via the app. The system doesn't wait to be certain — it acts on the pattern that matters most: silence when there should be movement.

Family Peace-of-Mind Snapshot

A family member opens the Zakey App during the afternoon and sees the home's status at a glance: motion logged in the living room at 2:15 PM, temperature steady at 22 °C, front door locked, curtains open. No intrusion, no cameras, no live feed — just the daily pattern confirming that everything is normal. The peace of mind of a check-in call, available without disturbing anyone.

Why it matters

Built around dignity and independence

  • Passive Safety Monitoring

    Presence sensors track daily routines without cameras or wearables. Unusual patterns trigger quiet family alerts before a situation becomes a crisis — proactive, not reactive.

  • Comfort Without Effort

    Climate, lighting, and curtains adapt automatically through the day. The resident lives in a consistently comfortable environment without managing a single device or setting.

  • Family Peace of Mind

    The Zakey App gives family members a daily pattern view — not surveillance, but reassurance. Home status at a glance replaces constant check-in calls and background worry.

  • Independent Living, Extended

    Smart automation removes the daily friction of managing a home — controls that are hard to use simply stop being used. Zakey keeps independence practical, not just aspirational.

  • Instant Emergency Response

    Smart lock remote access, pattern-break alerts, and door/window sensor notifications compress the response time from an event to a caring intervention — minutes instead of hours.

  • One-Touch Simplicity

    The Scene Button and voice commands replace complex app navigation entirely. Morning, evening, and rest scenes activate with a single physical press — accessible regardless of dexterity or digital confidence.
